What are your real odds?

The lottery prints the overall odds of winning anything — usually a free ticket or a couple of dollars back. It does not tell you the odds of winning a prize that's actually worth more than the ticket. So we worked them out, using the prizes still unclaimed and the tickets still unsold for every current scratch game.

How this is calculated

For each game we take the total number of tickets printed (from the game's own page on the lottery site) and multiply by the percent of tickets still unsold (from the lottery's unclaimed-prizes table) to estimate how many tickets are still out there to buy. For every top prize level, we divide those remaining tickets by the number of that prize still unclaimed:

odds of winning = (tickets printed × percent unsold) ÷ prizes still unclaimed

These are estimates and assume the unsold tickets are distributed like the rest of the pool — which the lottery does not guarantee. "Unclaimed" prizes may already be sitting in someone's drawer. Games whose individual page has been taken down by the lottery can't be matched and are not shown. This site is not affiliated with or endorsed by the Maine State Lottery.
